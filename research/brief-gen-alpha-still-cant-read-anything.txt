This video should argue that Gen Alpha's reading collapse is not just a school problem or a temporary post-COVID dip. The core angle is that this may be the first generation being raised inside a culture that no longer expects literacy as a baseline. The story should show that falling reading scores, teacher testimony, classroom chaos, screen saturation, chronic absenteeism, and lowered institutional expectations are all part of the same larger shift: children are being trained to consume fragments, visuals, and algorithmic entertainment before they ever build the habits required for reading, attention, and comprehension.

The piece should not become a generic "kids these days" rant. It should feel sharp, specific, and culturally diagnostic. The strongest version of the argument is that reading is collapsing because the entire environment around children now rewards the opposite of reading: short-form video, permanent distraction, passive device use, social promotion, and systems that adapt downward instead of forcing recovery. The script should make clear that Gen Alpha's literacy problem is not just about test scores. It is about whether a generation can follow complex ideas, sit with difficult text, understand institutions, and function independently in an adult world.

The first half should gather hard evidence and vivid proof points. Hunt for direct teacher testimony, literacy and NAEP data, reports on middle school or high school students reading far below grade level, the role of absenteeism, and evidence that many students now resist or avoid sustained reading entirely. The second half should widen into the deeper cultural argument: what happens to a society when reading becomes optional, when adults lower the bar to keep systems moving, and when technology quietly replaces literacy with audiovisual dependence. The ending should make the modern-day relevance explicit. This matters because Gen Alpha may be the first cohort entering adolescence in a genuinely post-literate media environment, and if that trend locks in, it will reshape education, work, politics, attention, and social trust for years.
